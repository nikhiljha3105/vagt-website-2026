Security & Safety Audit

Professional on-site assessments that identify security vulnerabilities before incidents occur. Every audit produces a scored, structured report with prioritised findings and a clear remediation roadmap.

What We Audit

Six domains.
No gaps.

Physical, operational, and safety risks are assessed systematically — not spot-checked. Every domain is reviewed against industry standards and VAGT's own internal benchmark, developed from 25 years of operational security experience.

Physical Security & Perimeter

Boundary control, site entry points, and on-ground physical protection assessed against standard protection levels.

  • Boundary fencing and gate condition
  • Perimeter lighting adequacy
  • Unauthorised entry point identification
  • Guard post placement and coverage

Access Control & Identity

Physical and electronic access management — from badge systems to visitor protocols — reviewed for gaps and misuse risk.

  • Badge, biometric, and RFID systems
  • Visitor registration and logging
  • Key and master access management
  • Tailgating and piggyback risk

Fire & Safety Compliance

Extinguisher placement, hydrant access, fire exit adequacy, and evacuation plan review against NBC and local fire department standards.

  • Extinguisher placement and inspection dates
  • Hydrant access and flow adequacy
  • Fire exit routes and signage
  • Evacuation drill records and plan

CCTV & Electronic Systems

Camera placement, blind spot identification, recording retention, and overall electronic surveillance system condition and coverage.

  • Camera placement and blind spot map
  • Recording retention and quality check
  • Alarm systems and response protocols
  • System maintenance and hardware condition

EHS & Industrial Safety

Environment, health, and safety review covering PPE compliance, chemical storage, electrical safety, and operational risk exposure.

  • PPE availability and compliance
  • Hazardous material storage and labelling
  • Electrical safety and earthing checks
  • Waste disposal and spill containment

Emergency Preparedness

ERT team readiness, BCP documentation, first aid adequacy, and incident command protocols assessed for practical effectiveness.

  • Emergency response team (ERT) structure
  • BCP documentation and currency
  • First aid kit availability and training
  • Incident reporting and command protocols
How We Work

A four-step process
from brief to report.

Every VAGT audit follows the same disciplined process — from scoping to delivery. You always know what to expect, and the report lands within 48 hours of the on-site visit.

01

Pre-assessment brief

We begin with a structured conversation — understanding your facility layout, past incidents, current security arrangements, and the specific concerns driving the audit request. This scoping step ensures the assessment is targeted, not generic.

02

On-site physical inspection

VAGT's certified inspector walks the entire facility systematically against a standardised 60-point checklist covering all six audit domains. Observations are recorded with photographic evidence where relevant. No area is skipped — including back-of-house, loading bays, and service corridors typically missed in informal reviews.

03

Report compilation and scoring

Findings are categorised by severity — Critical, Moderate, and Advisory — and scored against a 100-point composite index with per-domain breakdowns. Each finding is supported by the inspection evidence and paired with a specific corrective action.

04

Debrief and remediation roadmap

The completed report is delivered within 48 hours of the on-site visit. Our inspector walks through the findings with your team, clarifies priorities, and hands over a remediation roadmap with suggested timelines and responsible party recommendations for each corrective action.
